DATASET
-------
- 3 courses: CS 288 (Berkeley), CS 601 (JHU), CS 224N (Stanford)
- 55 total lectures, ~3,772 slides processed
- 5 question categories: text_only (TO), image_diagram (ID), table (TB),
  chart_graph (CG), layout_aware (LA)
- 150 curated QA pairs per course (450 total), lecture-balanced
- Automated pipeline: PDF → slide images → VLM draft generation → auto-filter → curation
- QA draft pool: ~16,930 total drafts across 3 courses

BASELINES (DONE, results in slideqa/data/results/)
---------------------------------------------------
- Closed-Book (question only, no context) — lowest bound
- Text-Only (BM25 + GPT-4o, no vision)
- Oracle VLM (correct slide image + GPT-4o) — upper bound

  Course       | Closed-Book F1 | Text-Only F1 | Oracle VLM F1
  -------------|----------------|--------------|---------------
  CS 288       |         0.0884 |       0.1426 |        0.3771
  CS 601       |         0.0895 |       0.1864 |        0.3725
  CS 224N      |         0.0774 |       0.1001 |        0.3940

PER-CATEGORY BREAKDOWN (DONE, results in slideqa/data/results/)

  CS 224N (text-only F1=0.062 vs VLM=0.393 — 6x gap).
- text_only questions are where text-only retrieval is closest to the VLM.

LM-AS-JUDGE (DONE, results in slideqa/data/results/)
----------------------------------------------------
- Script: slideqa/src/run_judge.py
- GPT-4o scores each prediction on a 1-5 scale vs gold answer
- All 3 courses × 3 baselines scored (900 judge calls total)
- Incremental caching: safe to interrupt and resume

  Course   | closed_book | text_only | zero_shot_vlm
  ---------|-------------|-----------|---------------
  CS 288   |       2.087 |     1.793 |         4.700
  CS 601   |       2.033 |     2.113 |         4.420
  CS 224N  |       1.807 |     1.533 |         4.600

- Key finding: judge scores confirm oracle VLM is strongly superior.
  closed_book outscores text_only on CS 288 and CS 224N — retrieval
  is actually hurting when the retrieved context is wrong.
